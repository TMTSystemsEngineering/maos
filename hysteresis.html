DM Hysteresis

The DM hysteresis modeling is based on "Modeling the hysteresis of a scanning probe microscope" J. Vac. Sci. Technol. B 18 (2), Mar/Apr 2000

Formula (2) is used with V replaced by command x, and x replaced by actual DM position y 

dy/dx=alpha*sign(dx)*(b*x-y)+(beta-u)

For each step with a new x, the position y is updated as 

dx=x-xlast
dy=alpha*abs(dx)*(beta*xlast-ylast+sign(dx)*(beta-u)
ylast+=dy
y=ylast

Both x and y are normalized by the DM stroke. Beta affects dy at x=+/-1, but does not effect dy at x=0. Alpha and u affects affects both dy at 0 and extreme. For different amount of hysteresis, the amount alpha can be kept constant, and we solve for u and beta to meet the boundary conditions: 

y=+/-1 at x=+/-1
y=+/-hysteresis at x=0

at x=0, y=+/- (u/alpha)*(1-2/(exp(-alpha)+exp(alpha))) at x=+/-1, y=+/- (beta-u/alpha*(1-2*exp(-alpha)/(exp(-alpha)+exp(alpha))))

From this, we can solve u and then beta.

Alpha=2 is a good value. Smaller alpha has slower convergence of the hysteresis curve. Alpha effects the hysteresis amount for difference sized loops.

Alpha is proportional to the stroke in un-normalized case.
